5. Digital certificate and insurance cover

5.1. Nature of the certificate

A StampScaner digital certificate is a document issued by the Founder confirming the results of expertise on a specific postage stamp or philatelic item. The certificate carries a unique registry number, an item description, an APS grade, a valuation range, the Founder's digital signature (Ed25519), and a record in the public registry at stampscaner.com/cert.

5.2. Certificate variants

  • Pre-cert — issued from Postman's automated analysis of photographs supplied by the User. No insurance cover. Marked "Pre-cert" both on the document and in the registry. Not intended for use in large deals, auctions or insurance claims.
  • Premium — issued after in-person expertise of the physical specimen, delivered to StampScaner or a partner laboratory. Carries an insurance cover of USD 1,000 subject to section 5.3.

5.3. Conditions of the Premium cover

The USD 1,000 cover is paid to the User in case of a fully demonstrated mismatch between the certificate information and the actual item (for example, the certified specimen turns out to be a counterfeit while the Service has not flagged it as doubtful), provided all of the following are met:

  • the valuation was ordered and paid as a paid service;
  • the specimen was delivered to StampScaner or a partner laboratory for in-person expertise;
  • the certificate was handed to the User personally or via confirmed delivery with verified recipient identification;
  • the claim is filed within 24 months of issue;
  • the specimen is presented for re-expertise in the same condition.

5.4. Exclusions

  • the certificate is a Pre-cert (no cover applies);
  • the User provided distorted or incomplete information;
  • the item was damaged, altered or restored after issue;
  • the claim concerns subjective market-value aspects beyond the APS grade;
  • the certificate was obtained by a third party without proper transfer of rights;
  • the claim contradicts a second independent expertise from a recognised body (PSE, APEX, BPA Expertising, the Philatelic Foundation, an AIEP expert).

5.5. Implementation of cover

As of the current version, cover is backed by a dedicated reserve account of the Founder. Following the signature of an agreement with a partner insurance company (planned in Sprint 5 of the Service roadmap), cover will be implemented through a licensed insurer. Users are notified of the transition 30 days in advance.

5.6. Refusal to issue

The Service may refuse to issue a certificate without explanation and shall refund the User minus actually incurred costs of shipping and preparation. Grounds for refusal include substantiated suspicion of forgery, dubious provenance, breach of cultural-heritage rules, sanction restrictions, or third-party claims on the item.
